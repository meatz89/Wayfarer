## THE CORE PRINCIPLE: Queue Order Creates Everything

The game centers on an **8-slot letter queue** where delivery order is sacred. This single constraint - you must deliver from position 1 or burn tokens - creates the entire strategic and emotional experience. Every letter represents a relationship, every position matters, every deadline conflicts.

## WAYFARER - Letters and Ledgers: YOUR LIFE IN 8 SLOTS

You are a letter carrier in a medieval world where every delivery strengthens or weakens a relationship. Your queue of 8 letters isn't just a task list - it's a visualization of all your social obligations, competing priorities, and impossible promises. The queue order rule transforms simple delivery into agonizing priority management.

## THE LETTER ECOSYSTEM

### How Letters Enter Your World

**The Morning Posting** (Main source):
- 3-5 letters appear on public boards each morning
- Visible attributes: Sender type, pay, deadline, distance
- Hidden attributes: True purpose, complications, consequences

**Direct Approaches** (Relationship-based):
- NPCs with 3+ connections to you offer private letters
- "My cousin needs this delivered, I'd only trust you..."
- These letters often pay less but deepen relationships

**Network Referrals** (Connection-spending):
- Spend 2 connections of any type: "Anything heading north?"
- Spend 3 matching connections: Request specific sender type
- NPCs remember who helped them - create future letter chains

**Obligation Generation** (Automatic):
- Standing Obligations spawn letters directly into queue
- Patron sends monthly intelligence requests (slot 1-3)
- Merchant Favor creates weekly trade runs
- Heart Bond generates "visit me" letters

### The Queue as Living Puzzle

**Queue Manipulation Tools**

**Morning Planning** (Free daily action):
- Examine all letters in detail
- Swap any two adjacent letters
- Spend 1 connection: Additional swap

**Crisis Management** (Connection costs):
- **Purge** (3 connections): Remove bottom letter
- **Priority** (5 matching): Move to slot 1
- **Extend** (2 matching): +2 days deadline
- **Skip** (1 matching): Deliver out of order

**Active Management** (Time/resource costs):
- **Focus Session** (half day): Move one letter directly to slot 1
- **Delegate** (2 matching connections): Remove low-value letter
- **Negotiate** (3 different connections): Extend any deadline by 2 days
- **Break Faith** (permanent cost): Purge all letters from one source

### Connection Generation Through Play

**Basic Actions Generate Connections**:
- Deliver letter on time: 1 connection (type matches recipient)
- Share meal/drink: 1 Common connection  
- Keep secret: 1 Shadow connection
- Attend church: 1 Scholar connection
- Win tavern game: 1 Common + 1 Shadow
- Help with problem: 2 matching connections
- Romance scene: 2 Heart connections

**Connection Types** (5 total):
- **Trust (Heart)**: Personal bonds, friendships, romance
- **Trade (Merchant)**: Commercial relationships, guild contacts
- **Noble (Court)**: Aristocratic connections, formal standing
- **Common (Folk)**: Everyday people, local knowledge
- **Shadow (Black)**: Underworld contacts, forbidden knowledge

### The Strategic Onion

**Layer 1: Route Optimization**
- Queue order suggests travel path
- But deadlines force suboptimal routes
- Connections unlock shortcuts
- Equipment enables dangerous paths

**Layer 2: Relationship Investment**  
- Which NPCs to develop into connection engines?
- Common folk give many weak connections
- Nobles give few powerful connections
- Balance depends on your strategy

**Layer 3: Obligation Management**
- Each Standing Obligation helps AND constrains
- When do you break obligations vs manage them?
- Some obligations conflict with each other
- Breaking faith has permanent consequences

**Layer 4: Long-term Positioning**
- Today's helped NPC becomes tomorrow's letter source
- Reputation with types affects letter quality
- Romantic subplot provides unique opportunities/crises
- Your patron's mysterious agenda slowly reveals itself

## QUEUE-BASED LOGICAL RULES

### Queue Position Rules
- **Position 1** = Must deliver next (or spend connections to skip)
- **New Letter + No Connections** = Enters at slot 8
- **New Letter + 3-4 Connections** = Enters at slot 7
- **New Letter + 5+ Connections** = Enters at slot 6
- **Patron Letter** = Jumps to slots 1-3 regardless

### Connection Cost Rules
- **Skip Delivery** = 1 matching connection per letter skipped
- **Extend Deadline** = 2 matching connections for +2 days
- **Purge Letter** = 3 any connections to remove
- **Emergency Priority** = 5 matching connections to jump to slot 1
- **Break Faith** = Lose all future letters from that source

### Deadline Pressure Rules
- **Letter Deadline 0** = Letter expires, sender relationship damaged
- **Multiple Deadlines Same Day** = Mathematical impossibility
- **Travel Time + Queue Order** = Creates route conflicts
- **Rest Need + Urgent Letters** = Health vs delivery dilemma

### Standing Obligation Rules
- **Noble's Courtesy** = Noble letters enter at 5, can't refuse nobles
- **Merchant's Priority** = Trade letters pay +10, can't purge trade
- **Shadow's Burden** = Triple pay shadow letters, forced shadow every 3 days
- **Heart Bond** = Trust letters extend free, double cost to skip

## CORE QUEUE MECHANICS: THE LIVING SYSTEM

### The Queue Is Your Life
**8 slots that visualize every obligation:**
- Position 1-8 determines delivery order
- Must deliver from slot 1 (or spend connections)
- Each letter has sender, deadline, payment, type
- New letters always enter at slot 8
- Delivered letters removed, others move up

### Connection Tokens Are Everything
**Five types representing different social spheres:**
- **Trust (Heart)**: Personal bonds, romance, friendship
- **Trade (Merchant)**: Commercial relationships, guild contacts
- **Noble (Court)**: Aristocratic connections, formal standing
- **Common (Folk)**: Everyday people, local knowledge
- **Shadow (Black)**: Underground contacts, illicit networks

**Token Uses:**
- Unlock routes (1 token at specific location)
- Purge letter (3 any tokens)
- Priority move (5 matching tokens)
- Extend deadline (2 matching tokens)
- Skip delivery (1 matching token per skip)

### Standing Obligations Shape Your Character
**Permanent modifiers that tell your story:**
- **Noble's Courtesy**: Noble letters at slot 5, can't refuse nobles
- **Shadow's Burden**: Triple shadow pay, forced shadow letters
- **Patron's Eye**: Patron letters advance 1 slot per day
- **Heart's Bond**: Personal letters can jump queue positions
- **Merchant's Favor**: Trade letters +10 coins, tighter deadlines

### The Patron Mystery
**Monthly letters with unclear purpose:**
- Always jump to slots 1-3 on arrival
- Vague requests: "Report on Baron's movements"
- High payment but mysterious goals
- Patterns emerge through multiple letters
- Your compliance affects future resources

**The result**: A living system where every mechanical decision creates story. Queue management isn't abstract optimization - it's choosing between love and money, friendship and obligation, immediate needs and long-term relationships. The game makes you feel like Kvothe: brilliant but overwhelmed, always juggling more than you can handle, serving powers you don't understand while trying to maintain the relationships that matter.
