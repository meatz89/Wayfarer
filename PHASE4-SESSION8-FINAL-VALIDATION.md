## Success Metrics Testing

### 1. "Make 50 Coins in 14 Days" Challenge ⏳
**Goal**: Validate challenge demonstrates strategic optimization gameplay

**Starting Conditions**:
- Location: millbrook/millbrook_market
- Starting capital: 12 coins
- Starting equipment: Trade Tools
- Inventory: 4 base slots
- Target: 50 coins by end of Day 14

**Expected Outcomes**:
- ✅ Challenge is achievable through multiple strategic approaches
- ✅ Requires strategic planning and optimization
- ✅ Cannot be achieved through random or unoptimized play
- ✅ Different strategies achieve success with different trade-offs

### 2. Multiple Strategy Validation ⏳
**Goal**: Confirm different approaches can achieve success

**Strategy A: Equipment Master Approach**
- Day 1-3: Complete contracts to earn 15-20 coins
- Day 4-5: Commission Climbing Gear and Navigation Tools (10 coins)
- Day 6-14: Access all 8 routes for maximum contract/trade flexibility
- Expected result: 50+ coins through diverse opportunities

**Strategy B: Trade Optimization Approach**
- Day 1-2: Acquire cart transport for +3 inventory slots
- Day 3-14: Focus on high-volume trading between markets
- Limited to 3 cart routes but maximize cargo efficiency
- Expected result: 50+ coins through bulk trading

**Strategy C: Contract Specialization Approach**
- Day 1-3: Build reputation with specific NPCs
- Day 4-14: Focus on renewable contracts from those NPCs
- Mix of equipment and trading based on contract needs
- Expected result: 50+ coins through contract optimization

**Expected Validation**:
- ✅ Each strategy can achieve 50 coins target
- ✅ Different strategies create different gameplay experiences
- ✅ No single dominant strategy exists
- ✅ Player preference drives strategic choice

### 3. Trade-off Recognition Testing ⏳
**Goal**: Validate players understand perfect optimization is impossible

**Key Trade-offs to Test**:

**Equipment vs Capital**:
- Equipment investment reduces trading capital
- Equipment enables route access and contract types
- Players must balance immediate vs future benefits

**Route Access vs Cargo Capacity**:
- Cart provides +3 slots but blocks 5 of 8 routes
- Equipment enables routes but consumes inventory slots
- Players cannot maximize both simultaneously

**Time vs Money**:
- Rush contracts (15 coins) require equipment and time pressure
- Standard contracts (8 coins) provide steady but slower income
- Equipment commissioning consumes valuable time blocks

**Expected Recognition**:
- ✅ Players understand they cannot have everything
- ✅ Trade-offs feel meaningful and impactful
- ✅ Optimization requires sacrificing some opportunities
- ✅ Strategic tension emerges from impossible perfection

### 4. Emergent Complexity Validation ⏳
**Goal**: Confirm simple systems create deep strategic decisions

**Simple Systems**:
- Inventory: 4 base slots (7 with cart)
- Equipment: 3 types unlock route access
- Contracts: 4 types with different requirements
- Time: Limited daily blocks force prioritization

**Emergent Complexity Examples**:

**A. Weather-Equipment-Route Interactions**:
- Fog blocks wilderness routes without Navigation Tools
- Rain blocks exposed routes without Weather Protection
- Simple rules create dynamic route availability

**B. NPC-Contract-Equipment Chains**:
- Workshop Master offers Craft contracts requiring Trade Tools
- Trade Captain offers Rush contracts requiring Climbing Equipment
- Simple categories create strategic contract planning

**C. Time-Space-Resource Optimization**:
- Limited inventory forces equipment vs cargo decisions
- Limited time blocks force activity prioritization
- Limited stamina forces efficiency optimization

**Expected Emergence**:
- ✅ Complex strategic situations from simple rules
- ✅ Players develop personal optimization approaches
- ✅ Strategic depth feels discovered, not prescribed
- ✅ Replayability through strategic experimentation
